Buyer Context

Why wholesalers look for GetCallers alternatives

They want a wholesaling-specific operating lane

GetCallers is often evaluated as an outsourced calling or call-center style option. That can be useful for buyers who need phone capacity. Wholesalers usually need more than phone time: list workflow, seller qualification rules, call notes, callback discipline, CRM stages, and a clear handoff to acquisition.

They want less owner-side setup

A cold caller only becomes useful when the dialer, data, scripts, dispositions, and follow-up process are already defined. VA Horizon is built for owners who do not want to assemble Readymode, HighLevel, QA, and reporting as separate projects after hiring the caller.

They need a defined lead standard

For wholesale acquisitions, a lead is not only a name and phone number. The caller should capture motivation, condition, timeline, asking price context, property notes, and the correct next action. Ask every provider how it defines a qualified lead before comparing price.

Best for investors who prefer a real estate VA agency with broader role coverage.

REVA Global is a real estate virtual assistant provider, so it is more relevant to investors than a general offshore staffing marketplace. MyOutDesk

Best for buyers who want a large dedicated VA company across multiple business functions.

MyOutDesk is a broad virtual assistant company with a long-running presence in real estate and other industries. It can make sense when a team wants a dedicated VA for multiple functions or wants a larger staffing brand. For wholesalers, the tradeoff is specialization. Ask exactly how cold calling scripts, list workflow, call review, CRM setup, dialer access, and lead handoff are handled. MyOutDesk may be a fit for broader staffing. VA Horizon is built for a narrower outbound seller-acquisition machine.

5

Freelance callers from Upwork or Fiverr

Best for hands-on owners who want maximum hiring control and accept the management work.

Freelance marketplaces can work when the operator already knows how to recruit cold callers, test voice quality, run role plays, write scripts, configure the dialer, build the CRM, monitor calls, and replace weak performers. The apparent monthly cost can look lower, but the owner supplies the missing management layer. Freelance is not a bad route for a founder who wants control and has time. It is a poor fit when the business needs predictable outreach volume quickly and the owner is already the acquisition manager.

Decision Notes

How to choose without comparing the wrong things

Do not compare only the hourly caller cost. The real comparison is total monthly operating cost: caller, dialer, CRM, list workflow, call review, missed follow-up, replacement time, and owner supervision. VA Horizon's $1,160 single-VA price includes the VA and Readymode dialer fee. It also includes the HighLevel buildout that many owners otherwise have to pay for or configure themselves.

Ask each provider to define the exact handoff. A useful seller lead should tell your acquisition person why the seller might sell, what condition the property is in, how fast the seller wants to move, what price context came up, and what the next step should be. If the provider only reports activity, you still own the hard part of turning activity into pipeline.
