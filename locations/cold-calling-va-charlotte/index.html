Frequently Asked Questions

Is Charlotte a competitive wholesale market?
Charlotte is one of the Southeast's most active wholesale markets. Rapid population growth, a large banking and finance employment base, and significant in-migration have created strong investor demand. The outer suburbs — Gaston, Union, and Cabarrus counties — still offer motivated seller density with less competition than the urban core.
What zip codes are best for motivated sellers in Charlotte?
Top cold calling territories include West Charlotte (28208, 28216), Gastonia (28052, 28054), Concord (28025, 28027), and Matthews/Mint Hill (28105). These areas combine absentee owner density with accessible price points for wholesalers.
Is wholesaling legal in North Carolina?
North Carolina requires careful structuring for wholesaling. Assigning contracts for real property may require a real estate license if you are regularly engaged in these activities. Many NC wholesalers use double-close strategies or work with a licensed agent. Consult a North Carolina real estate attorney before wholesaling at scale.
