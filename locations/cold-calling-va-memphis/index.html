Market Context

Why Memphis Wholesalers Need Cold Calling VAs

Memphis is routinely ranked among the top five wholesale markets in the US - a combination of low prices, strong rental demand from landlords and Section 8 operators, and high absentee owner rates across older neighborhoods. The market rewards volume: the investor who reaches motivated sellers first sets the price.

▲

Low Entry Prices = More Deals

Memphis median prices around $210K with suburban comps as low as $80–140K mean wider spreads and lower MAOs - ideal conditions for consistent wholesale deal flow at scale.

▲

Dense Absentee Owner Inventory

Whitehaven, Hickory Hill, Frayser, and North Memphis have among the highest absentee owner rates in Tennessee - making them prime cold calling territories for any active wholesaler.

▲

Strong Rental and Landlord Demand

Memphis has a large Section 8 housing market and one of the highest renter-occupied rates in the South - driving consistent buyer demand from local and out-of-state landlords.

▲

Speed Determines Deals

Motivated sellers in Memphis often field calls from multiple investors in the same week. Cold calling volume and follow-up speed directly determine which wholesaler gets the contract.

Is Memphis a good market for real estate wholesaling?
Memphis is one of the top wholesale markets in the Mid-South. High rental demand, significant absentee owner inventory, low median home prices, and a strong cash buyer pool make it one of the most consistent deal-flow markets in the country.
What zip codes should I target for motivated sellers in Memphis?
High-yield areas include Whitehaven (38116), Hickory Hill (38141/38115), North Memphis (38107/38108), Frayser (38127), and Raleigh (38128). These neighborhoods have high absentee ownership rates, older housing stock, and consistent motivated seller density.
Does Tennessee have restrictions on real estate wholesaling?
Tennessee does not require a real estate license to wholesale properties via contract assignment, provided you are marketing your equitable interest - not the property itself. Always consult a local real estate attorney to ensure your assignment agreements are compliant with current Tennessee statute.
