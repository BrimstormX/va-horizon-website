Pain Points

Where Land Investors lose time and deals

Land lists require patient volume

Landowners may be hard to reach and slow to respond. Consistent calling and follow-up increase the odds of finding sellers who are ready.

Parcel details matter

Acreage, access, utilities, zoning, terrain, and county rules can change whether a lead is worth reviewing.

Follow-up is where many land deals surface

Owners may not sell immediately. A CRM process keeps future sellers sorted by motivation, price expectation, and parcel fit.

Workflow Fit

How VA Horizon fits the operating model

Land investors need a slightly different intake process than house wholesalers. VA Horizon can structure campaigns around county lists, absentee owners, inherited land, and long-hold parcels. The caller collects owner interest and basic parcel context, while the operator evaluates comps, access, zoning, and exit strategy.

Daily operating rhythm

The team starts with a clean campaign brief, a defined lead standard, and one place for every conversation to land. Callers focus on live seller conversations, managers review the quality of those conversations, and the CRM tells the operator what needs attention next. That rhythm keeps outreach from becoming a pile of disconnected notes.

Owner handoff standard

A useful VA system does not just say someone is interested. It captures why they may sell, when they want to move, what is happening with the property, what the next step should be, and who owns that step. That handoff standard is what lets the operator make faster decisions without replaying every call from scratch.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can this support county-specific land campaigns?
Yes. The workflow can be organized by county, acreage range, owner type, and follow-up stage.
Do callers analyze zoning or access?
No. Callers collect basic seller and parcel information. The operator should evaluate zoning, access, utilities, and comps.
Is SMS useful for land sellers?
It can be useful for follow-up, but consent, opt-outs, and campaign registration need to be handled carefully.
What role should a land investor hire first?
Most start with a caller, then add lead management when follow-up volume becomes too high to manage personally.
