PropStream Review for Real Estate Wholesalers (2026)

Verdict: 4.4/5. PropStream is a strong fit for real estate wholesalers who need targeted property lists and have a clear process for turning records into outreach. VA Horizon uses PropStream in the list and skip workflow because it gives VAs access to 150M+ property records, useful filters such as absentee owners, vacant properties, liens, and pre-foreclosure, plus built-in DNC scrubbing before outreach. It is not a complete acquisition system by itself. Lists still need filter discipline, export hygiene, skip tracing coordination, dialer formatting, CRM tagging, and campaign review. PropStream is best for operators who know their market and want cleaner list pulls. It is weaker for owners who expect software to decide motivation without human review.

Who It Is For

PropStream is strongest when the operator knows what list they want

PropStream gives wholesalers a broad property data layer, but broad data is useful only when the operator has a list thesis. A VA can pull absentee owners with equity, vacant properties, lien records, pre-foreclosure signals, tired landlord targets, or other filters. That does not mean every record is worth calling. The filter stack has to match the market, offer strategy, calling capacity, and compliance process.

For VA Horizon, PropStream sits near the start of the workflow. The VA or operations team pulls lists, screens obvious mismatches, runs DNC scrubbing, prepares the export, coordinates skip tracing when needed, and then routes cleaned data into the dialer and CRM process. The cold calling VA should not be building lists during the calling shift. That separation matters because a caller's job is to call, qualify, and submit. VA Horizon handles list sourcing and skip tracing coordination around that role, then the list moves into the Readymode dialing workflow.

The strongest PropStream users treat list pulls as experiments with feedback, not one-time downloads. If absentee owner lists are producing low contact rates, the team can tighten geography, adjust equity rules, add ownership-length filters, or test vacant property signals. If pre-foreclosure pulls create sensitive conversations but few qualified sellers, the team can adjust the script and compliance review before increasing volume. PropStream gives the raw targeting layer. The operator still has to learn which signals produce conversations in that market.

Firsthand Pros and Cons

What VA Horizon likes and where PropStream needs process

Pros

  • Large property data set: The 150M+ record base gives VAs a broad research layer across U.S. markets.
  • Wholesaling-friendly filters: Absentee, vacant, lien, pre-foreclosure, equity, ownership, and property filters support targeted list pulls.
  • Built-in DNC scrubbing: The DNC step helps reduce obvious risk before records move toward outreach.
  • Good export workflow: Lists can move into skip tracing coordination, dialer prep, and CRM tagging when the columns are handled cleanly.
  • Useful market research: The platform helps the team inspect neighborhoods, owner signals, and property context before dialing.

Cons

  • Filter overload: Too many filters can produce a list that looks precise but has too little call volume.
  • Data is not motivation: A vacant or absentee signal does not prove the owner wants to sell.
  • Exports still need cleanup: Duplicates, missing fields, bad formatting, and wrong market choices can slow the dialer workflow.
  • Skip tracing still matters: A property list is not the same thing as clean reachable phone numbers.
  • Pricing changes: Buyers should verify current PropStream pricing, limits, data terms, and add-ons directly.
Pricing Note

Verify PropStream pricing, limits, and add-ons directly

PropStream pricing, export limits, data access, add-ons, skip tracing terms, trial details, and included features can change. Buyers should verify current pricing directly with PropStream before deciding. The practical budget question is larger than the monthly software cost. A wholesaler also needs time for list strategy, filter setup, export cleanup, skip tracing coordination, DNC scrubbing, dialer prep, and CRM tagging.

VA Horizon's view is simple: list software is useful, but a calling operation needs a list process. VA Horizon handles list sourcing and skip tracing coordination around the caller, then routes the data into Readymode and HighLevel. That keeps the cold calling VA focused on conversations instead of spending the shift deciding which properties to export.

There is also a hidden management cost when owners pull lists themselves without a weekly review loop. Bad filters can fill the dialer with owners who do not match the campaign. Duplicate exports can waste caller time. Missing source tags can make reporting useless. Weak DNC handling can create avoidable risk. VA Horizon keeps those tasks outside the caller's daily role so the operator can judge the campaign by actual seller conversations rather than by how many records were downloaded.

That review loop is where PropStream becomes more valuable. The team can compare list source, contact rate, wrong-number rate, seller motivation, and lead quality, then change the next pull instead of blaming the caller for a weak data set.

How VA Horizon VAs pull and prepare lists with PropStream

1

Define the market

The team starts with market, county, ZIP targets, property type, owner profile, equity posture, and the campaign goal.

2

Select filters

VAs use filters such as absentee, vacant, liens, pre-foreclosure, equity, ownership length, property type, and location.

3

Review obvious issues

The export is checked for mismatched properties, weak criteria, bad columns, duplicates, and records that do not fit the campaign.

4

Scrub and trace

The list goes through DNC scrubbing and skip tracing coordination when phone data needs to be appended or improved.

5

Prepare outreach

Records are formatted for Readymode and tagged for HighLevel so callers can work without guessing lead source or list type.

6

Use call feedback

Contact rate, wrong numbers, motivation, and property feedback are used to tighten future list pulls instead of repeating weak filters.
