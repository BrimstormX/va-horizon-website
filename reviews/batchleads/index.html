Who It Is For

BatchLeads is strongest when the team owns list operations

BatchLeads makes the most sense when a wholesaler has a list thesis and enough outreach volume to justify organized data work. A VA can build absentee owner, vacant, equity, foreclosure, tired landlord, cash buyer, and other list segments, but the value depends on filter discipline. A broad database can produce bad campaigns when the operator downloads everything and sends it straight to a caller. The stronger workflow is narrower: define the buy box, pull the right records, enrich or skip trace missing contact details, remove duplicates, review compliance fields, and export the list into the outreach system.

The reason VA Horizon treats BatchLeads as an operations tool rather than a magic lead source is simple. A cold calling VA should not spend the calling shift building lists, fixing columns, deduping records, or guessing which contact number belongs in the dialer. VA Horizon separates the workflow. List sourcing and skip tracing coordination happen before the caller starts. Then the caller works the cleaned list, qualifies seller motivation, and submits real opportunities. That is also why BatchLeads should be compared with managed list sourcing, not only with another software login.

BatchLeads is also useful for operators who need to stack lists. A record that appears on one broad absentee list may not be strong enough to prioritize. A property that overlaps with vacancy, equity, ownership length, tax delinquency, and a clear market fit deserves more attention. The tool can help expose those overlaps, but a person still has to decide what goes into the next campaign and what gets held back.

Best fit

Wholesalers building repeatable lists, stacking distress signals, and exporting data for steady calling or SMS campaigns.

Poor fit

Operators who want a tool to choose motivation without market knowledge, VA review, or campaign feedback.

Managed fit

Clients who want VA Horizon to handle list sourcing, skip tracing, formatting, and campaign handoff before outreach starts.

Firsthand Pros and Cons

What VA Horizon likes and where BatchLeads can add work

Pros

  • One list workspace: BatchLeads can keep property records, owner data, saved lists, uploads, and exports closer together than a spreadsheet-only process.
  • Useful filter depth: Public pages list more than 140 customizable filters, which helps VAs build targeted campaigns instead of broad city dumps.
  • Bulk upload support: Imported records can be enriched and organized, which is useful when a client has county data, old CRM lists, or another vendor export.
  • List stacking value: Overlap across absentee, vacant, equity, tax, and ownership signals gives the team a better prioritization layer.
  • Operational fit: It fits a VA-led workflow where list building, skip tracing, and export prep are handled before the caller starts dialing.

Cons

  • Data cost details move: Plan limits, export limits, add-ons, and per-record costs can change. Verify current BatchLeads pricing directly.
  • Still needs list judgment: More filters do not automatically mean a better list. Market knowledge and campaign feedback still decide quality.
  • Exports can get messy: Bulk work creates duplicate records, stale numbers, missing fields, and source confusion if the VA does not enforce naming rules.
  • Not the final outreach system: Cold calling still needs dialer formatting, number workflow, dispositions, call QA, and CRM follow-up.
  • Compliance is separate: List tools do not remove the need for DNC review, campaign rules, opt-out handling, or legal review for SMS and calling.
Pricing Note

Verify BatchLeads pricing, data limits, and add-ons directly

BatchLeads pricing should be checked directly before purchase. Public pricing pages may list plan tiers, monthly lead allowances, export allowances, user counts, add-ons, and in-app purchase costs for extra records or exports, but those details can change. Do not rely on an old comparison article for final numbers. Verify the current plan, contract term, monthly record limits, import rules, export limits, skip tracing terms, extra record pricing, and any SMS, dialer, or direct-mail add-ons directly with BatchLeads.

For VA Horizon clients, the more important question is the cost of the whole lead operation. A data platform is only one line item. The operator still needs someone to define lists, pull records, run or coordinate skip tracing, clean exports, tag sources, load the dialer, manage DNC review, and connect the campaign to HighLevel. When those steps fall to the owner, the software can look cheap and still create hours of work every week.

VA Horizon Workflow

How VA Horizon uses BatchLeads-style list workflows

1

Define the list thesis

VA Horizon starts with market, property type, seller signal, equity logic, owner profile, and outreach channel. The list has to support the offer strategy.

2

Pull and stack records

VAs build or import records, stack signals, remove obvious mismatches, and keep source labels so later performance can be traced back to the list.

3

Coordinate skip tracing

The team enriches missing owner phones and emails, checks field quality, and avoids handing callers a list full of blanks or duplicate contacts.

4

Prepare outreach files

Exports are formatted for Readymode, HighLevel, or SMS campaigns with clean columns, source tags, owner names, property addresses, and campaign labels.

5

Protect the caller role

The cold calling VA's job is to call, qualify, and submit. The VA should not lose shift time fixing skip trace output or rebuilding list filters.

6

Feed campaign learning

Contact rate, lead rate, seller objections, wrong numbers, and do-not-call issues feed back into the next list pull so the campaign gets sharper.
