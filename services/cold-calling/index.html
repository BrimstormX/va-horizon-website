Role Boundaries

What this service does and does not own

What the role does

The VA has one job: call seller records, qualify motivation, capture property context, and submit qualified leads. The caller does not build lists, skip trace, tag CRM records, or manage admin work.

What VA Horizon manages

VA Horizon handles list sourcing coordination, skip tracing workflow, Readymode setup, HighLevel buildout, scripts, QA, follow-up sequences, and weekly performance management.

What the client still owns

The client reviews qualified leads, makes offer decisions, handles legal and contract review, and closes the deal or routes it to acquisitions.

HighLevel CRM, configured for wholesaling

Weekly performance review

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as a "qualified lead"?
A qualified lead is a seller who confirmed motivation to sell, gave property details (condition, situation, timeline), and agreed to a next step: whether that's an appointment, a callback, or a specific follow-up date. The caller does not submit anyone who said "maybe call me back sometime." Every lead in your CRM has a reason it's there.
How fast can we actually get to first dial?
Most clients are live within 48 to 72 hours of the intake call, assuming your list is ready or we're coordinating list sourcing in parallel. The CRM and dialer are configured during that window. The main delays are usually on the client side: access to HighLevel, list delivery, or finalizing the seller profile.
Do I need to provide lists or does VA Horizon handle that?
VA Horizon coordinates list sourcing and skip tracing through preferred partners. If you already have lists from BatchLeads, PropStream, or another source, we can use those too. What the caller needs is a skip-traced, phone-appended list. How it gets sourced is flexible based on your setup.
Does this include HighLevel, or do I need my own account?
HighLevel is included and configured for your pipeline. You don't need an existing account. If you already have one with prior data or automations, we can work within your existing setup, but you won't need to build anything yourself.
What happens if the VA underperforms or leaves?
VA Horizon manages replacements within 5 business days at no additional cost. Because the system (scripts, CRM, campaign brief) is already set up, a new VA can get productive quickly. You're not starting from scratch every time a seat turns over.
Do I still need to make offers and close deals myself?
Yes. The cold calling seat creates qualified seller conversations. What you do with those conversations is still your call. If you want a VA handling initial seller follow-up and offer prep, that's the acquisitions manager role, a separate engagement. Cold calling alone gets leads into your pipeline. You decide what to do with them.
