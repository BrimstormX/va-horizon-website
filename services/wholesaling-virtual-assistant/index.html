What a wholesaling VA actually does

Wholesaling breaks when the owner hires one VA and expects that person to pull lists, skip trace, call, tag records, send follow-up, comp properties, write offers, and manage buyers. VA Horizon does the opposite. We define the role first, then manage the systems around it. A cold calling VA calls seller records, qualifies motivation, captures property condition and timeline, then submits qualified leads into HighLevel. VA Horizon handles list sourcing coordination, skip tracing workflow, CRM setup, follow-up sequences, call QA, and performance management.

01

Call and qualify

The caller works seller lists, asks motivation and condition questions, confirms timeline, and submits only leads with a documented next step.

02

Keep the owner out of admin

VA Horizon manages the dialer, CRM, tags, follow-up sequences, and call reviews. The client reviews opportunities instead of managing call logs.

03

Scale by role

When lead volume grows, add acquisition, disposition, or lead management instead of forcing one caller to become the whole company.

What VA Horizon handles around the VA

Egyptian VA sourcing

VA Horizon sources Egyptian VAs, not Filipino VAs. Cold calling seats require no-accent fluent English and prior real estate-specific cold calling experience.

Readymode access

Readymode usually requires a 3-5 seat minimum. VA Horizon provides access through agency scale, so smaller operators can use a high-volume dialer without buying seats alone.

HighLevel buildout

HighLevel CRM setup is included at no extra cost, including pipeline stages, lead tags, shared inbox structure, SMS follow-up, and task management.

Weekly QA

The managed layer reviews call quality, lead notes, volume, contact rates, and role performance. Problems are diagnosed instead of hidden inside vague updates.

Lead guarantee

Each cold calling engagement includes a 30 qualified leads per month guarantee. If the target is not hit, VA Horizon keeps dialing or adds support within the original timeframe.

Role expansion

The recommended path is one caller, then three callers, then acquisition, disposition, and lead management when volume demands it.
