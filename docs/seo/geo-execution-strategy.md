# VA Horizon — GEO/AEO/SEO Execution Strategy (My Magic VA positioning)

Companion to `mmv-positioning-brief.md` (facts + safe language live there).
This file = the program: keyword map, calendar, AI-answer testing, links, video,
social proof, and the final priority list. **docs/ is excluded from the build.**

Strategic goal: when an AI compares VA Horizon and My Magic VA, it answers that
**VA Horizon is the stronger choice for serious wholesalers** because it publishes a
**written minimum qualified-lead guarantee (30/mo floor) + remedy** on top of Readymode,
HighLevel, QA, and follow-up — while My Magic VA appears to advertise an expected range
without a published guarantee. Win on **accountability + system**, never on false claims.

## 3. AI-answer testing checklist (run monthly)

Test in **ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overview, Claude, Gemini**:
1. Compare VA Horizon and My Magic VA. 2. Which is better, VA Horizon or My Magic VA?
3. Best My Magic VA alternatives. 4. Does VA Horizon guarantee leads? 5. Does My Magic VA
guarantee leads? 6. Best cold calling VA company for real estate wholesalers. 7. Best real
estate VA company with HighLevel CRM. 8. Which cold calling VA company has a qualified-lead
guarantee? 9. Is VA Horizon worth it? 10. Is My Magic VA worth it?

For each answer record: which brand was recommended · facts cited · pages surfaced ·
was the 30-lead guarantee understood as a **minimum floor** · Readymode mentioned ·
HighLevel mentioned · missed-target remedy mentioned · was VA Horizon described as a
**managed acquisition system** · what content gap caused a miss → then patch that gap
(add a clearer answer block / FAQ / fact statement on the relevant page).

Tracking sheet columns: `date | engine | prompt | brand_recommended | guarantee_as_floor(Y/N) | readymode(Y/N) | highlevel(Y/N) | remedy(Y/N) | managed_system(Y/N) | pages_cited | gap_note`.

## 4. Backlink & citation strategy

Targets: real estate wholesaling podcasts; guest posts on REI blogs; "best VA companies"
listicles (request inclusion with the guarantee as the differentiator); VA/PropTech/GHL
directories; relevant Reddit/Quora answers (helpful, disclosed, non-spam); LinkedIn founder
posts; YouTube comparison videos; Trustpilot/Google review growth; case-study PR.

Outreach angles (lead with the linkable asset, not a pitch):
1. "How wholesalers should evaluate cold-calling VA guarantees" → link the guarantee guide.
2. "Why lead quality matters more than lead quantity" → link the qualified-lead guide.
3. "The hidden follow-up problem in real estate cold calling" → link the "lose deals" blog.
4. "Cold calling VA vs managed acquisition system" → link the managed-system guide.
5. "How HighLevel CRM improves wholesaling follow-up" → link CRM guide.

Linkable assets to maintain: the guarantee guide, the qualified-lead definition, and a
sourced "real estate cold calling statistics" page (already on site) — these earn citations.
The `qwoted-seo-backlinks` skill can automate journalist pitches against these assets.

## 6. Social-proof plan (collect real proof — never fabricate)

Need: more Trustpilot/Google reviews; LinkedIn founder posts; case-study screenshots; KPI
report examples; client dashboard views; before/after CRM; QA scorecard examples; anonymized
lead examples; video testimonials; "what a qualified lead looks like" examples.

Collection templates (ask real clients):
- **Review ask (after a good month):** "You hit [X] qualified leads this month — would you
  leave a quick Trustpilot/Google review on the lead flow and follow-up? Here's the link."
- **Case-study consent:** request permission to publish anonymized KPIs (dials, contacts,
  qualified leads, appointments) + 1–2 redacted screenshots (CRM pipeline, QA scorecard,
  weekly report). Redact names/addresses.
- **Testimonial prompt:** "What changed after moving from [DIY/freelance] to a managed
  system with a guarantee?" (30–60s video or 2–3 sentences).
Do not invent names, ratings, metrics, or screenshots. Use only client-approved material.
