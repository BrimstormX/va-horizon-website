Dispo Follow-Up That Moved More Contracts: 21 Days → 6 Days

A Tampa wholesaler was sitting on contracts for three weeks because nobody was working the buyer list. The deals were real. The follow-up process was the bottleneck. A dedicated dispo VA and SMS automation cut the timeline to under a week.

The Situation

Terrence had been wholesaling in Tampa for four years. His acquisition side was solid. He had a cold calling VA and was putting 2 to 3 properties under contract every month. The problem was on the back end: getting those contracts assigned to a buyer took three weeks on average. Sometimes four.

His dispo process was entirely manual. Terrence would blast his buyer list via email, wait a few days, text a couple people, and close with whoever called back first. When he was busy with contract negotiations or walkthroughs, dispo sat untouched for days.

The cash flow impact was real. A 21-day dispo cycle meant capital locked up in earnest money for weeks at a time. Faster dispo would free up working capital and let him take on more deals per month. But only if someone was actually running the process.

Dispo Process Before VA Horizon

  • ✕No dedicated dispo manager. Terrence handled all buyer outreach himself.
  • ✕Buyer outreach was a single email blast. No segmentation, no follow-up sequence.
  • ✕No SMS component. Buyers were expected to respond to email.
  • ✕Inconsistent follow-up timing. Some buyers heard back after 5 days, some after 12.
  • ✕No buyer engagement tracking. No visibility into who opened, clicked, or responded.

How We Fixed the Dispo Pipeline

Two things: a dedicated dispo manager and automated SMS sequences triggered the moment a property went under contract. We didn't change Terrence's buyer list or his deal criteria.

Step 1: Buyer List Segmentation in HighLevel

We imported Terrence's full buyer list into HighLevel (GHL) and tagged contacts by property type preference (SFR vs. multi-family), price range, and geographic submarket. Instead of blasting the entire list for every deal, the dispo manager could target only the buyers most likely to be interested in a specific property.

Step 2: Automated SMS Blast on Contract Signature

We built a HighLevel automation: the moment a deal moved to "Under Contract" in the pipeline, a targeted SMS blast fired to the relevant buyer segment. The message included the address, property details, ARV estimate, asking price, and a direct link to a one-page deal summary. Buyers could respond by text within minutes.

Step 3: Dispo Manager Follow-Up Sequence

Our dispo manager ($1,440/mo, $9/hr) took over after the initial SMS blast. Any buyer who responded got a call within 2 hours. Non-respondents received a follow-up SMS at 24 hours and 48 hours with different framing. By day 3, the dispo manager had a warm list of interested buyers and was working deals toward assignment before Terrence had to get involved.

Step 4: Warm Handoff to Terrence

When a buyer confirmed interest and was ready to sign, the dispo manager flagged the lead as a warm handoff in HighLevel with all negotiation notes. Terrence got a summary and took the closing call. His involvement went from 100% of the dispo process to the final 20%.

The Results

The first deal on the new system was assigned in 4 days. The second took 5. By month 2, the average settled at 6 days. That's a 71% reduction from 21 days. Terrence closed more deals per month with the same acquisition team because his working capital recycled faster.

Why Dispo Bottlenecks Cost More Than Acquisition Problems

Acquisition gets all the optimization attention. Better scripts, more callers, fresher lists. Dispo usually doesn't get touched until it becomes an obvious problem. But a 21-day assignment cycle means capital locked in earnest money for three weeks per deal. You can only run so many deals in parallel before the math breaks.

Cutting dispo time by 70% doesn't require better deal flow. It frees up capacity you already have. When you clear a contract in 6 days instead of 21, you can take on roughly 3.5x as many contracts before hitting the same ceiling. That's what happened for Terrence. Same acquisition team, same list, more monthly closings because deals moved faster. A dispo manager at $1,440/mo ($9/hr) paid for itself on the first deal.

The channel shift matters too. Email open rates in real estate investor circles run around 20% on a good day. SMS open rates for property deals regularly exceed 90%, and response rates for segmented, targeted offers hit 30% or higher. Moving from bulk email to segmented SMS through HighLevel is the single biggest lever in dispo speed.
