One cold caller to a full acquisition team

Problem

Hubbard Housing Realty wanted to grow outbound, but the owner had no interest in spending his weeks posting jobs, running interviews, and building training decks just to add seats.

Implementation

Started with one caller in August 2025. Once it produced, we added callers two and three, an acquisition manager, a part-time dispo VA, and an SMS blast - one seat at a time, each added only after the last was working.

What Changed

1 Caller → Full Acquisition Team

One HighLevel build running the whole operation

Problem

Cash Offers Today, a two-partner cash-offer operation in California, was running on a stack of disconnected tools - a dialer here, an answering service there, leads in a sheet, contracts in a folder - with nobody owning the full picture.

Implementation

Built one HighLevel system for everything: two callers and an acquisition manager on a shared pipeline, a 24/7 AI inbound agent, and contracts generated inside the CRM. The partners configure none of it.

Implementation

Put an AI voice agent, Emily, on the inbound line. She answers in seconds around the clock, qualifies the caller, and writes a full recap into HighLevel as a workable lead.

Replacing a VA over a single weekend

Problem

Isam came from an agency that billed $10/hr for an untrained general VA he had to bring his own dialer and lists for. His first caller with us had a weak opening week.

Implementation

Instead of asking him to wait it out, we swapped the caller between Friday and Monday. Same setup, new caller, no re-onboarding and no extra charge.

NTX Property Group: 18% to 92% answered or captured

The line was not quiet. It was ringing at bad times: during appointments, after dinner, and on weekends. Too many paid inbound calls turned into blank missed-call logs.

Implementation

We moved the number into HighLevel, used AI answering for overflow and after-hours, and gave the AM a morning queue with names, numbers, addresses, and plain call notes.

Gulf Coast Property Group stuck at one deal a month

Problem

Closing a deal kept pulling the owner away from the work that created the next one. A strong contract week often left the following week thin on fresh seller conversations.

Implementation

We put three Egyptian real-estate callers on Readymode, kept their submissions in one GHL pipeline, reviewed calls weekly, and added the AM after the lead volume justified it.

1 Deal/Month → 4 Deals/Month

LMA Holdings contracts sitting too long after signing

Problem

Contracts were getting signed, then sitting. The owner had buyers, but the list was full of old names, loose JV contacts, and people whose real buy boxes lived in memory.

Implementation

We had a disposition VA clean the list, tag buyers by county, price band, rehab appetite, and POF, then work a daily follow-up sequence for each contract.

Memphis follow-up buried in notes and old tags

Problem

The CRM was full of half-useful notes: probate timing, tenant issues, high asking prices, and "call me later" records. Nobody trusted it enough to work it every day.

Implementation

We forced every old record into a real outcome: dead, future follow-up, or active callback. Then a lead manager worked that queue instead of leaving it in the owner's head.

What Changed

Old Notes → Daily Follow-Up Queue

Vacant land records with no reply system

Problem

Patrick Juettner had county records and a price-per-acre model, but seller replies were scattered across texts, calls, email, spreadsheets, and handwritten reminders.

Implementation

We handled SMS setup, A2P registration, list prep, and reply routing so each response landed in HighLevel with county, acreage, APN, asking price, and review notes.

Katie Callen's AM drowning after the callers started working

Problem

The caller team started producing enough leads that the AM became the filter. Too much of his day went to weak submissions, missing property details, and sellers who were months away.

Implementation

We added a lead manager to clean up submissions, fill missing fields, warm up future sellers, and move only AM-ready conversations to the senior closer.

The Same Playbook Every Time

None of this was a lucky one-off

Different operators, different bottlenecks, one method underneath: wholesaling-trained people, a system they plug into, and QA that catches problems before you feel them.

01

Trained for Wholesaling, Not Generic VA Work

MAO math, seller-motivation qualifying, skip tracing, CRM hygiene, objection handling. The foundation is the business itself, not generic VA training bent to fit it.

02

Weekly QA and Real Accountability

Calls are scored every week and the numbers come to you. If a caller isn't performing, we replace them within five business days. You never run the hire-and-train cycle yourself.

03

A System, More Than a Seat

A caller alone doesn't move the needle. The VA plugs into a built HighLevel CRM, SMS follow-up, and pipeline automation, so the whole outreach machine works as one.
