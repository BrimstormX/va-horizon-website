Case Study: Lead Management

From Missed Leads to Clean Follow-Up: 4x ROI in 60 Days

A Phoenix wholesaler was losing deals because nobody was working the leads after the first call. No CRM, no follow-up system, one person doing everything. We fixed all of it in two months.

TL;DRA Phoenix wholesaler went from 1.5x to 4x ROI in 60 days. We deployed one cold calling VA on Readymode, built his HighLevel CRM, and set up automated SMS follow-up. Same list budget. Same ad spend. The only thing that changed was the system behind it.

The Situation

Marcus had been wholesaling in Phoenix for two years. He was pulling lists, skip tracing, and cold calling on his own. Around 80 to 100 touches per week. He was closing maybe one deal a month, sometimes zero.

On our intake call, the problem was obvious: no follow-up system. Leads came in from cold calls, text blasts, and direct mail. Marcus logged some in a spreadsheet, called a few back when he had time, and lost the rest. He guessed he was following up with less than 30% of his leads.

Marcus was the only person doing everything. Calling, texting, entering data, negotiating. No VA. No CRM built for his workflow. Just a list, a phone, and a spreadsheet. That works at low volume. At 80+ touches per week, deals move faster than one person can track.

Key Problems Before VA Horizon

  • ✕No follow-up sequence. Leads fell off after the first call.
  • ✕No CRM. Leads tracked in a Google Sheet with inconsistent columns.
  • ✕No dedicated caller. Marcus handled outbound himself between negotiations.
  • ✕No SMS follow-up after initial calls. Zero automated re-engagement.
  • ✕About 70% of generated leads were never contacted a second time.

What We Built

We ran a full intake and systems audit in the first week. Marcus didn't need more leads. He needed to actually work the ones he was already paying to generate. So we focused on plugging the leaks before anything else.

Week 1-2: HighLevel CRM Buildout

We migrated Marcus's Google Sheet into a fully configured HighLevel (GHL) pipeline with 7 stages: New Lead, Attempted Contact, Conversation Started, Appointment Set, Offer Sent, Under Contract, Closed/Dead. Every contact record got disposition tags, property details fields, and a follow-up timeline. VA Horizon handled the entire buildout. Marcus didn't touch any of it.

Week 2-3: Cold Caller Deployed

We assigned Marcus an Egyptian VA with fluent, no-accent English and prior real estate cold calling experience. The VA's job was simple: call, qualify, and submit leads. VA Horizon handled the list sourcing, skip tracing, CRM tagging, and follow-up sequences. The VA went through a two-day briefing on Marcus's market, typical seller motivations, and his ARV targets before dialing. Within 48-72 hours of onboarding, the VA was live on Readymode (predictive dialer), hitting 800+ dials per day with 150-200 connections per day.

Week 3: SMS Blast Integration

Every lead that didn't answer the first call got enrolled in a 3-touch SMS sequence through HighLevel: a same-day text, a 48-hour follow-up, and a 7-day re-engagement. We used A2P-compliant messaging registered to Marcus's business number. No shared pools, no carrier filtering risk.

Week 4: Reporting Dashboard Live

Marcus got a live dashboard showing daily dials, contacts made, appointments set, and pipeline value by stage. For the first time, he could see exactly where every lead stood and where deals were stalling. That alone changed how he spent his mornings.

The Results

By week 8, Marcus's operation looked completely different. We didn't increase his list size or ad budget. The improvement came from working the leads he was already paying for through a repeatable system.

Why Lead Recovery Beats Buying More Lists

The average solo wholesaler follows up with fewer than 40% of the leads they pay to generate. We see this on almost every intake audit. Operators spend on list pulls and skip tracing, then leave more than half their leads unworked because there's nobody assigned to follow up.

Marcus went from working 30% of his pipeline to 100%. A dedicated VA on Readymode handled all outbound calling and qualification (800+ dials per day with 150-200 connections per day). VA Horizon handled list sourcing, skip tracing, CRM management, and the follow-up sequences. HighLevel gave every lead a defined next action. Automated SMS covered the non-answers that used to disappear.

None of that required more leads. It required working the ones already in the system. At $960/mo for the VA and $200/mo for the Readymode dialer seat, the math worked from month one.
