TL;DRIn 60 days, a Phoenix wholesaler increased ROI from 1.5x to 4x by deploying a VA Horizon cold calling VA and HighLevel CRM — without changing his list source or ad spend.

The Situation

Marcus had been wholesaling in Phoenix for two years. He was generating enough leads — roughly 80 to 100 inbound and outbound touches per week — but he was closing only one deal per month on average, sometimes zero. His pipeline felt like a leaky bucket.

When we did the intake call, the problem was immediately obvious: there was no follow-up system. Leads came in from cold calls, text blasts, and direct mail. Marcus would log a few in a spreadsheet, call some back manually, and lose track of the rest. He estimated he was following up with less than 30% of the leads he generated.

The deeper issue was that Marcus was the only person doing everything — calling, texting, entering data, and negotiating. No VA. No CRM built for his workflow. A list, a phone, and a spreadsheet. That setup can work when volume is low. At 80+ touches per week, it's a structural problem: deals move faster than one person can track them.

Key Problems Before VA Horizon

  • ✕No structured follow-up sequence — leads fell off after the first call
  • ✕No CRM — leads tracked in a Google Sheet with inconsistent columns
  • ✕No dedicated caller — Marcus handled outbound himself between negotiations
  • ✕No SMS follow-up after initial calls — zero automated re-engagement
  • ✕Estimated 70% of generated leads were never contacted a second time

What We Built

In the first week, we ran a full intake and systems audit. We identified that Marcus's biggest leverage point wasn't generating more leads — it was recovering the leads he was already paying to generate. The entire Phase 1 of our engagement focused on plug-and-stop: stop the leak before scaling.

Week 1-2: HighLevel CRM Buildout

We migrated Marcus's Google Sheet into a fully configured HighLevel pipeline with 7 stages (New Lead → Attempted Contact → Conversation Started → Appointment Set → Offer Sent → Under Contract → Closed/Dead). Every contact record included disposition tags, property details fields, and a follow-up timeline.

Week 2-3: Cold Caller Deployed

We assigned Marcus a trained, accent-neutral cold caller from our roster with direct real estate wholesaling script experience. The VA went through a two-day product briefing covering Marcus's market (Phoenix metro), typical seller motivations, and his ARV targets before dialing a single number.

Week 3: SMS Blast Integration

Every lead that didn't answer the first call was automatically enrolled in a 3-touch SMS sequence via HighLevel: a same-day text, a 48-hour follow-up, and a 7-day re-engagement. We used A2P-compliant messaging registered to Marcus's business number — no shared pools, no carrier filtering risk.

Week 4: Reporting Dashboard Live

Marcus got a live dashboard showing daily dials, contacts made, appointments set, and pipeline value by stage. For the first time, he could see exactly how many leads were in each stage and where deals were stalling. This alone changed how he prioritized his time each morning.

The Results

By the end of week 8, Marcus's operation looked fundamentally different. Not because we increased his list size or ad budget — we didn't touch either. The improvement came entirely from capturing leads he was already generating and working them through a repeatable system.

Why Lead Recovery Outperforms List Expansion

The average solo wholesaler follows up with fewer than 40% of the leads they pay to generate. That number isn't a guess — it shows up consistently in intake audits. Most operators are spending on list pulls and skip tracing, then leaving more than half their leads unworked because there's no system behind the outreach.

This case study shows 4x ROI from the same list spend because Marcus went from working 30% of his pipeline to working 100% of it. Three things made that possible: a dedicated caller handling all outbound and qualification, a CRM configured to give every lead a defined next action, and automated SMS covering the non-answers that would otherwise fall off completely.

None of that requires more leads. It requires actually working the ones already in the system.
