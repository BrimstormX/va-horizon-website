Short Answer

Short answer: Before hiring a cold-calling VA company, ask whether the qualified-lead number is a guaranteed minimum with a remedy or just an expectation, how they define a qualified seller lead, and whether the dialer, CRM, QA, and follow-up are included and managed. VA Horizon answers yes to each and publishes a 30-lead floor.

Guarantee Questions

Start With Accountability

The best cold-calling VA company is not the one with the biggest advertised number. It is the one with the clearest lead definition, CRM process, QA, and performance accountability.

1

Do you guarantee a minimum number of qualified leads?

A strong provider should make the lead number clear as a minimum floor, not a vague expectation. VA Horizon publishes minimum 30 qualified leads/month, guaranteed.

2

What happens if the minimum is missed?

Ask for the remedy before you sign. VA Horizon's policy is: if we miss the floor, we keep dialing at no additional charge or add caller support until it's met.

3

How do you define a qualified seller lead?

A qualified seller lead is more than a phone contact. Confirm name, property address, decision-maker status, motivation, timeline, price expectation, condition notes, next step, and CRM tag.

For the full definition, read what counts as a qualified seller lead. To understand the performance floor, read the real estate cold-calling VA guarantee guide.

Dialer and CRM

Make Sure Tools Are Included and Managed

4

Is the dialer included?

Ask whether Readymode is included. VA Horizon includes Readymode with its cold-calling VA plan.

5

Is CRM setup included?

A CRM only helps if the provider builds it around the pipeline. VA Horizon builds and manages HighLevel CRM.

6

Do you manage HighLevel after setup?

Ask who owns pipeline stages, automated SMS follow-up, shared inbox, lead tagging, and task management after launch.

VA Horizon's cold calling VA service includes Readymode, HighLevel CRM, QA, KPI reporting, and follow-up as part of the managed outbound system.

QA and Lead Control

Ask How Lead Quality Is Protected

7

Are calls recorded?

Call notes or a recording where possible help confirm what happened on the call and whether the lead was actually qualified.

8

Who reviews quality?

Weekly QA means calls are reviewed and scored, and KPI reporting shows whether the caller and campaign are improving.

9

How are leads tagged?

Lead tagging matters because without a proper CRM stage or tag, a record may be only a contact, not a qualified seller opportunity.

Follow-Up Ownership

Ask What Happens After the First Call

10

Who follows up after the first call?

For wholesalers, the follow-up after the call is often where deals are won or lost. Ask whether follow-up is managed or left to manual cleanup.

11

What seller notes are captured?

Strong seller notes include motivation, timeline, price expectation, property condition, occupancy status where available, and the agreed next step.

12

What happens when a caller underperforms?

Replacement support matters. VA Horizon provides replacement support if a VA underperforms.

Scale and Fit

Ask Whether the Provider Can Scale Beyond One Caller

13

What is the replacement policy?

Ask how the provider handles a VA who misses performance expectations. The answer should be clear before the campaign starts.

14

Do you specialize in real estate wholesaling?

VA Horizon is a managed virtual-assistant company for real estate wholesalers and provides trained cold-calling VAs for real estate investors.

15

Can the system scale beyond one caller?

Scaling starts with one accountable caller and a clean pipeline. VA Horizon's path is cold caller to lead manager to acquisition manager or disposition manager.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I choose the cheapest real estate cold-calling VA? +
Lowest price is rarely the best measure. A cheaper caller can cost more if leads aren't qualified, tagged, and followed up. Compare the lead definition, whether the qualified-lead number is a guaranteed minimum with a remedy or just an expectation, and whether the dialer, CRM, QA, and follow-up are included and managed. The best value is accountability plus a complete system.
What should I compare before hiring a real estate VA company? +
Compare: (1) whether they guarantee a minimum number of qualified leads and what happens if they miss; (2) how they define a qualified seller lead; (3) whether Readymode and HighLevel CRM are included and managed; (4) whether calls are recorded and reviewed; (5) how leads are tagged and followed up; (6) replacement policy; and (7) whether the system scales beyond one caller. VA Horizon publishes a 30-lead floor and answers yes across this list.
Does VA Horizon guarantee real estate leads? +
Yes. VA Horizon guarantees a minimum of 30 qualified leads per month per cold-calling engagement. This is a floor, not a ceiling. Typical output depends on list quality, market, contact rate, and offer. If a campaign misses the floor, VA Horizon continues dialing at no additional charge until the target is reached, or adds caller support to hit it within the original timeframe.
