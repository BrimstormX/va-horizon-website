Short Answer

Short answer: Most cold-calling VA companies advertise expected lead volume; few publish a guaranteed minimum with a remedy. VA Horizon guarantees a minimum of 30 qualified leads/month, a floor, not a ceiling, and if a campaign misses it, VA Horizon keeps dialing at no extra charge or adds callers until it's met.

Accountability

Claims vs Averages vs Targets vs Guarantees

Advertised lead volume is a marketing estimate; a guaranteed minimum is a written commitment. The best cold-calling VA company is not the one with the biggest advertised number. It is the one with the clearest lead definition, CRM process, QA, and performance accountability.

Term What It Means for a Wholesaler
Lead claim A public number that may describe expected lead volume or an advertised outcome.
Average A planning number that still depends on list quality, market, contact rate, and offer.
Target A campaign goal. It matters most when the provider states what happens if the target is missed.
Guarantee A written minimum performance floor with a defined remedy if the floor is missed.
Floor Not Ceiling

A Written Floor Is Not Expected Output

VA Horizon publishes a written minimum performance floor, not a ceiling. The phrase to look for is minimum 30 qualified leads/month, guaranteed. Thirty is the guaranteed minimum floor, not the expected ceiling.

Typical output depends on list quality, market, contact rate, and offer. The operations playbook notes about 90+ qualified leads/month across three callers and that one acquisition manager handles 50+/month. The 30-lead figure exists so clients have a written, accountable performance standard, not a cap on results.

Expected vs Guaranteed

Why an Advertised Number Is Not a Written Guarantee

Ask whether a number is a guaranteed floor with a remedy, or just an expectation. An advertised lead number does not protect a wholesaler unless the provider also states the minimum floor and what happens if the campaign misses it.

A guarantee you can read beats a promise you have to assume. Accountability means the provider carries the risk of a slow month, not you.

Remedy

Why a Missed-Target Remedy Matters

A missed-target remedy is the difference between activity and accountability. If the floor is missed, the question becomes who carries the risk of an underperforming month.

VA Horizon's Missed-Target Remedy

if the target isn't met, VA Horizon (a) continues dialing at no additional charge until the target is reached, or (b) places additional VAs to hit the target within the original timeframe.

This is why VA Horizon describes its offer as a managed outbound acquisition system, not a caller-only service. A caller alone produces activity; the system around the caller produces a pipeline.

Qualified Leads

A Bigger Number of Weak Contacts Is Not Better

A larger advertised number of weak contacts is not the same as qualified seller leads. Lead quality is defined by what is captured on the call, not by the dial count.

A qualified seller lead includes the seller's name and contact, the property address, decision-maker confirmation, motivation, timeline, a price or pricing expectation, property-condition notes, occupancy status where available, an agreed next step, call notes or a recording where possible, and a proper CRM stage/tag.

Buyer Questions

Ask About Guarantee Terms Before Hiring

Before hiring a cold-calling VA company, ask whether the qualified-lead number is a guaranteed minimum with a remedy or just an expectation, how they define a qualified seller lead, and whether the dialer, CRM, QA, and follow-up are included and managed.
