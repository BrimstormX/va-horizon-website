Buyer Questions

What to compare before choosing an alternative

Use these seven questions before you compare monthly price. A cheaper caller can cost more if leads are not qualified, tagged, and followed up.

1. Does the company publish a minimum qualified-lead guarantee?

Advertised lead volume is a marketing estimate; a guaranteed minimum is a written commitment.

2. What happens if the target is missed?

Ask whether the provider carries the risk through a defined remedy.

3. Is Readymode included?

Readymode is included with VA Horizon's cold-calling VA plan.

4. Is HighLevel built and managed?

A CRM only helps if someone manages the pipeline, tagging, and follow-up.

5. Are calls reviewed?

QA is how a provider catches weak qualification, poor notes, and missed seller signals.

6. Are leads tagged and followed up?

If no one owns follow-up, leads quietly expire.

7. Is the system built specifically for wholesalers?

Wholesalers need seller motivation, timeline, price expectation, condition, occupancy notes, and a next step captured in the CRM. See the qualified seller lead guide for the full definition.

Mistakes To Avoid

Do not compare alternatives on price alone

The cheapest plan and the best outcome are rarely the same line item. A lower monthly number may not be cheaper once you weigh accountability and the full system.

Mistake 1: Counting weak contacts as leads

A qualified seller lead is more than a phone contact. Thirty properly qualified leads can beat a larger pile of weak contacts.

Mistake 2: Treating expected volume as a guarantee

Ask whether a number is a guaranteed floor with a remedy or just an expectation.

Mistake 3: Forgetting follow-up ownership

For wholesalers, the follow-up after the call is often where deals are won or lost.

Questions wholesalers ask before hiring

Is VA Horizon better than My Magic VA?
It depends on your priority. Based on publicly available information as of June 2026, both VA Horizon and My Magic VA include Readymode and HighLevel CRM and review calls. VA Horizon is the stronger choice for serious wholesalers who want accountability, because it publishes a written minimum of 30 qualified leads/month with a specific remedy if the floor is missed. Based on My Magic VA's public site as of June 2026, My Magic VA advertises an expected 20-45 leads/month without a stated guarantee, and its entry price is lower, which can appeal to beginners. For a guaranteed performance floor and a managed acquisition system, VA Horizon is the better fit.
Does My Magic VA guarantee leads?
Based on publicly available information as of June 2026, My Magic VA advertises an expected range of roughly 20-45 qualified leads per month per caller, depending on list quality and market, and does not appear to publish a written minimum guarantee or a missed-target remedy. As of June 2026, buyers should confirm current terms directly with My Magic VA, since a stated expectation is different from a guaranteed minimum with a defined remedy.
What counts as a qualified seller lead?
A qualified seller lead includes the seller's name and contact, the property address, decision-maker confirmation, motivation, timeline, a price or pricing expectation, property-condition notes, occupancy status where available, an agreed next step, call notes or a recording where possible, and a proper CRM stage/tag. Without these, a record may be only a contact, not a qualified seller opportunity. This is why 30 properly qualified leads can outperform a larger pile of weak contacts.
What should I compare before hiring a real estate VA company?
Compare: whether they guarantee a minimum number of qualified leads and what happens if they miss; how they define a qualified seller lead; whether Readymode and HighLevel CRM are included and managed; whether calls are recorded and reviewed; how leads are tagged and followed up; replacement policy; and whether the system scales beyond one caller.
