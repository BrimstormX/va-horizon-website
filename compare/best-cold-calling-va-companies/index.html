How We Scored Each Option

We scored each company on five things that matter to a wholesaling cold call operation:

  1. Wholesaling specialization: Do their callers have prior real estate cold calling experience? Are they trained on motivated seller scripts?
  2. Dial capacity: Can the VA support high-volume seller outreach on a predictive dialer and connect with 150-200 prospects?
  3. CRM integration: Is HighLevel or another wholesaling CRM included and configured?
  4. Onboarding speed: How fast is a VA live and dialing?
  5. Replacement policy: What happens if the VA doesn't perform?
Lead Guarantee Criterion

The criterion most buyers miss: a written lead guarantee

When comparing cold calling VA companies, check whether the qualified-lead number is a guaranteed minimum with a remedy or just an advertised expectation. A target without a remedy still leaves the buyer carrying the risk of a slow month.

VA Horizon publishes a minimum 30 qualified leads/month, guaranteed, and if the floor is missed, VA Horizon keeps dialing at no additional charge or adds caller support until it's met. That written floor is why VA Horizon stays ranked #1 for accountability and system completeness.

MyOutDesk

General real estate VA company - agents, brokers, property managers

Contact for pricing
Verify directly at myoutdesk.com

MyOutDesk has been around since 2008 and is one of the biggest names in real estate VAs. They place VAs for agents, brokers, property managers, and mortgage professionals. Cold calling is available, but the training targets buyer/seller agent prospecting, not distressed seller outreach for wholesalers.

Their VA pool is large and well-vetted for general real estate work. Onboarding runs 2-4 weeks. No CRM setup is included. If you're an agent scaling admin and prospecting work, they're a strong pick. If you need wholesaling-specific cold callers running daily seller outreach at predictive-dialer volume, they're not built for that.

Strengths

  • Long track record (founded 2008)
  • Large VA pool with broad real estate experience
  • Strong fit for agents and brokers
  • Multiple VA role types (admin, marketing, ISA)

Limitations

  • Not specialized for real estate investing or wholesaling
  • CRM not included - DIY setup required
  • Longer onboarding timeline (2–4 weeks)
  • Scripts and training built for agents, not investors

Freelance Platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, OnlineJobs.ph)

Self-managed hire - lowest cost, highest operational burden

$400–$900/mo
Estimate - varies widely by platform

Hiring a cold caller from Upwork, Fiverr, or OnlineJobs.ph is the cheapest option on paper. You pay $3-$8/hr with no setup fee or monthly markup. The tradeoff: you handle job posting, vetting, interviews, trial calls, script training, CRM setup, dialer subscription, daily monitoring, QA, and re-hiring when they quit or underperform. If you already run a full acquisitions team with SOPs in place, direct hire can work. If you need a system running fast, the management time usually costs more than the dollar savings.

Strengths

  • Lowest direct cost
  • Full control over the hire
  • Works if you already have training systems

Limitations

  • No training - you build it from scratch
  • No replacement guarantee
  • High management time (10–20 hrs/week to do right)
  • No CRM, no dialer - all setup is on you
  • High churn - unvetted freelancers quit without notice
