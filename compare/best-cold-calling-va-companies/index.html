Best Cold Calling VA Companies for Real Estate Wholesalers (2026)

We compared specialized agencies, general VA firms, and freelance platforms side by side. The ranking is based on what matters to a wholesaling operation: dials per day, caller experience, CRM setup, and what happens when a VA doesn't work out.

Disclosure: VA Horizon is included in this comparison and is the author of this page. All competitor data comes from public websites as of March 2026. Verify directly before purchasing.

TL;DR If you need Egyptian cold callers making 800+ dials per day with 150-200 connections, trained on wholesaling scripts, logging leads in your HighLevel CRM, and backed by a 30 qualified leads/month guarantee, VA Horizon is the only agency built for that. General VA companies like MyOutDesk work well for agents and brokers. Freelance platforms cost less upfront but you handle all the training, QA, and replacement yourself.

How We Scored Each Option

We scored each company on five things that matter to a wholesaling cold call operation:

  1. Wholesaling specialization: Do their callers have prior real estate cold calling experience? Are they trained on motivated seller scripts?
  2. Dial capacity: Can the VA hit 800+ dials per day on a predictive dialer and connect with 150-200 prospects?
  3. CRM integration: Is HighLevel or another wholesaling CRM included and configured?
  4. Onboarding speed: How fast is a VA live and dialing?
  5. Replacement policy: What happens if the VA doesn't perform?

Freelance Platforms (Upwork, Fiverr, OnlineJobs.ph)

Self-managed hire — lowest cost, highest operational burden

$400–$900/mo
Estimate — varies widely by platform

Hiring a cold caller from Upwork, Fiverr, or OnlineJobs.ph is the cheapest option on paper. You pay $3-$8/hr with no setup fee or monthly markup. The tradeoff: you handle job posting, vetting, interviews, trial calls, script training, CRM setup, dialer subscription, daily monitoring, QA, and re-hiring when they quit or underperform. If you already run a full acquisitions team with SOPs in place, direct hire can work. If you need a system running fast, the management time usually costs more than the dollar savings.

Strengths

  • Lowest direct cost
  • Full control over the hire
  • Works if you already have training systems

Limitations

  • No training — you build it from scratch
  • No replacement guarantee
  • High management time (10–20 hrs/week to do right)
  • No CRM, no dialer — all setup is on you
  • High churn — unvetted freelancers quit without notice
