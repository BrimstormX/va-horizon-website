OnlineJobs.ph lets you find affordable VAs directly. VA Horizon is a managed service that provides trained, accountable cold callers with systems already in place. The question isn't which is cheaper per hour - it's which actually produces pipeline for your wholesaling operation.

Disclosure: VA Horizon is the author of this page. OnlineJobs.ph data is sourced from their public website as of May 2026. We've aimed to be accurate and fair - verify directly before purchasing from either service.

VA Horizon is a managed service - your caller arrives trained, the dialer and CRM are set up, and an account manager handles oversight. Same labor pool, radically different operator burden.

The Real Differences Wholesalers Feel

1. The Hiring and Training Burden

VA Horizon

You don't hire anyone. You sign up, get onboarded in 48-72 hours, and your VA starts dialing your motivated seller list. Scripts, objection handling, CRM workflow, dialer setup - it's all done. Your job is to review leads and close deals.

OnlineJobs.ph

You become an employer. Post the job, review 50-200 applicants, interview 5-10 candidates, make an offer, handle onboarding, write training materials, set up the dialer access, configure CRM permissions, and then monitor performance daily for the first month. This process typically takes 3-6 weeks of real operator time before the VA is independently productive.

Wholesaler verdict: If you're running a high-volume operation and have the SOPs, training materials, and management bandwidth already in place, OnlineJobs.ph is a viable sourcing tool. If you're still building the machine while trying to generate deals, adding a hiring project on top is a significant drag.

3. What Happens When Your VA Leaves or Underperforms

VA Horizon

You message your account manager. Within 5 business days you have a replacement caller who is already trained on the same scripts and workflow. Your pipeline doesn't stop. You don't re-interview, re-train, or re-build. The replacement guarantee is included in your monthly fee.

OnlineJobs.ph

When your VA quits or stops performing, you restart the hiring process: re-post the job, sort through new applications, interview, select, onboard, and train again. This typically takes 2-4 weeks - during which your cold calling pipeline is completely stopped. The cost of this downtime is often several hundred to several thousand dollars in missed pipeline, depending on your operation's deal velocity.

Common Questions

Are OnlineJobs.ph callers better or worse than VA Horizon's Egyptian callers? +
Neither nationality is inherently better - it comes down to training, script quality, and accountability. VA Horizon specifically sources Egyptian callers who have been tested for neutral-accent English and prior cold calling experience. The key differentiator isn't geography - it's whether the caller has been trained on real estate wholesaling specifically and is held accountable with daily performance metrics.
Can I find a great cold caller on OnlineJobs.ph for $400/month? +
It's possible, but at $400/month you're typically looking at junior VAs with limited cold calling experience. Effective cold callers with real estate or sales backgrounds on OnlineJobs.ph typically ask $500-$800/month. Beyond salary, you still need to add a dialer ($99-$149), list-pulling tools, and your own training investment before they're productive. The all-in cost rises quickly.
Is there a scenario where OnlineJobs.ph makes sense for a wholesaler? +
Yes - for operators who have already built a complete training system, have a dialer setup and SOPs documented, and have internal bandwidth to manage and QA a VA daily. Scaling operations with 3+ callers also benefit from the cost savings once the management infrastructure is built. It's a strong option for established operators, a challenging one for those still building their systems.
How long does hiring through OnlineJobs.ph typically take? +
A typical hiring cycle on OnlineJobs.ph for a cold caller takes 1-3 weeks from posting to first dial - assuming you've already written job requirements, have interview questions ready, can move quickly on decisions, and have a dialer and training materials prepared. If any of those aren't in place, add time. VA Horizon's 48-72 hour onboarding eliminates the entire cycle.
Does VA Horizon require a long-term contract? +
No. VA Horizon is month-to-month with 30 days notice. OnlineJobs.ph gives you complete flexibility as an employer. The difference is that VA Horizon's switching cost is near zero - you cancel with notice and your pipeline keeps running until your last day. OnlineJobs.ph switching cost is the lost time and pipeline from restarting the hiring cycle if your VA doesn't work out.
