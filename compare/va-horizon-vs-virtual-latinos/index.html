VA Horizon vs Virtual Latinos for Real Estate Wholesalers

Direct answer: For real estate wholesalers comparing VA Horizon vs Virtual Latinos, the decision is bigger than country of talent. Virtual Latinos is best for companies that want to hire Latin American virtual professionals across admin, sales, marketing, finance, legal, real estate support, or bilingual customer-facing roles, then interview candidates and manage the daily work internally. VA Horizon is best for wholesalers who need a finished outbound acquisition system: dedicated Egyptian cold calling VAs with prior real estate calling experience, Readymode dialer access, HighLevel CRM buildout, QA, and a 30 qualified leads per month guarantee. Virtual Latinos can be a strong staffing path when you already have scripts, tools, managers, and a training process. VA Horizon fits operators who need the caller, dialer, CRM, lists, follow-up structure, and accountability owned together from week one.

Model

A managed wholesaling system versus a VA hiring path

The core difference is who owns the operating system after the hire starts.

VA Horizon model

VA Horizon sells a managed acquisition function for wholesalers. The client does not have to source dialer seats, build HighLevel, design the lead submission process, or supervise every call. VA Horizon handles list coordination, CRM stages, scripts, call review, weekly QA, and caller performance. The VA role stays narrow: call sellers, qualify motivation, collect property details, and submit qualified leads. That is useful when the operator wants predictable outbound volume without becoming the trainer, systems admin, and call coach.

Virtual Latinos model

Virtual Latinos is closer to a recruiting and staffing path for Latin American virtual professionals. Its public site presents many roles, including administrative support, sales and marketing, finance, HR, legal, medical, property management, and real estate assistance. The client shares requirements, interviews candidates, and then manages the person inside their business. That can work well when the client already knows the workflow and needs a person to plug into it.

Caller Fit

Real estate cold calling experience changes the ramp

Motivated seller calls are different from admin support, appointment setting, or general inside sales.

VA Horizon caller profile

VA Horizon sources Egyptian VAs, not Filipino VAs, and requires prior real estate cold calling experience before placement. The accent standard is no-accent fluent English for U.S. seller calls. The role is not general appointment setting. Callers need to handle defensive property owners, ask about timeline and condition, separate curiosity from motivation, and log the right details for acquisition follow-up. A caller who has already worked seller lists starts closer to production.

Virtual Latinos talent profile

Virtual Latinos is strongest when the buyer wants Latin American talent, timezone overlap, and possible English or Spanish capability across a broad role list. That does not make it weak. It means the buyer must confirm whether a specific candidate has wholesale real estate cold calling experience, seller script familiarity, dialer discipline, and comfort with rejection-heavy outbound work. Those details matter more than the broad VA category label.

Tools

Readymode and HighLevel are included in the VA Horizon package

The tools decision affects daily call volume, lead handoff, and management visibility.

VA Horizon stack

VA Horizon gives clients managed Readymode dialer access and a HighLevel CRM buildout at no extra setup cost. Readymode matters because it has a 3 to 5 seat minimum and is not usually practical for a solo operator hiring one freelancer. With the dialer and list workflow aligned, a trained caller can reach 800 to 1,000 dials per 8-hour shift. HighLevel then receives lead notes, stages, follow-up tasks, and SMS automation in a structure the team can manage.

Client-owned stack

With a staffing platform, the client normally supplies the dialer, CRM, scripts, phone numbers, permissions, reporting, and daily workflow. That can be fine if the business already has Readymode, HighLevel, call dispositions, and QA scorecards in place. If those pieces are missing, the assistant may spend the first weeks waiting on tools, asking how to tag leads, or dialing from a setup that cannot reach enough live sellers.

Pricing

Transparent managed cost versus role-based VA rates

The sticker price is only useful if it includes the system required to produce calls.

VA Horizon pricing

VA Horizon charges $1,160 per month for one cold calling VA, made up of $960 for the VA and $200 for the dialer. At three or more VAs, the price is $1,000 per month per VA, including the dialer. Acquisition manager and disposition manager roles are $1,440 per month, and a lead manager role is $1,120 per month. The included HighLevel buildout means the client is not buying a separate CRM implementation before the caller can start.

Virtual Latinos pricing

Virtual Latinos publishes role and experience-based monthly ranges on its own site, and those ranges can change. It is fair to treat that as staffing cost, not as a fully managed cold calling system price. A wholesaler should add the cost of dialer access, CRM buildout, list handling, skip tracing, script creation, management time, call review, and any consultant needed to make the assistant productive.

Accountability

The guarantee is tied to qualified leads, not hours worked

Wholesalers ultimately need motivated seller opportunities, not only a filled seat.

VA Horizon guarantee

VA Horizon guarantees 30 qualified leads per month per cold calling engagement. If the target is not met, VA Horizon continues dialing at no extra charge until the target is reached, or places additional VAs to hit the target within the original timeframe. That guarantee is possible because VA Horizon controls the caller, QA, dialer setup, CRM workflow, and daily management. The operator is not left alone to figure out why the seat is underperforming.

Staffing accountability

With a staffing path, accountability usually centers on the quality of the candidate, the replacement process, and support from the agency. The client still owns the operational outcome. If scripts are weak, the CRM is messy, the dialer is slow, or nobody reviews calls, the assistant can be capable and still fail to produce enough qualified seller leads. That is not a criticism of Virtual Latinos. It is the natural tradeoff of self-managed staffing.

Best Fit

The better option depends on what you already have built

A wholesaler with no outbound system needs a different purchase than a company with managers and tools already in place.

Choose VA Horizon when

Choose VA Horizon when cold calling is the main growth channel, the team needs daily seller conversations, and the operator wants one accountable partner managing caller performance, Readymode, HighLevel, QA, and lead submission. It is a better fit when the business is already committed to U.S. wholesaling and wants to move from manual follow-up to a managed pipeline.

Choose Virtual Latinos when

Choose Virtual Latinos when the need is broader than wholesaling cold calls. A business might want a bilingual executive assistant, customer support person, social media assistant, finance assistant, legal assistant, or real estate admin who works directly under internal leadership. It is also a stronger fit when the client values Latin American timezone overlap and already has strong managers in place.

Honest Fit

When Virtual Latinos is the better choice

Virtual Latinos is the better choice when you want Latin American talent specifically and you have the operating system ready. If your company already has a CRM, dialer, role documentation, scorecards, and a manager who can train the assistant, a staffing model can give you more hiring control.

It can also be the better choice for roles outside seller cold calling. A wholesaler might use Virtual Latinos for Spanish-speaking admin support, bookkeeping help, recruiting coordination, social media, transaction paperwork, or other support roles where broad business fit matters more than managed dial volume.

The most important question is not which company has better people. It is whether you want to own the process yourself. Virtual Latinos gives you a path to talent. VA Horizon gives you a narrower, managed acquisition system for wholesaling.
