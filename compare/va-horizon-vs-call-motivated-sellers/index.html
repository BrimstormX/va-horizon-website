VA Horizon vs Call Motivated Sellers

Direct answer: For real estate wholesalers comparing VA Horizon vs Call Motivated Sellers, the decision is mostly about control. Call Motivated Sellers can make sense when bilingual lead delivery matters more than owning the calling process. VA Horizon fits operators who want a caller they can scale into a full acquisition team, with 800 to 1,000 dials per 8-hour shift and launch in 48 to 72 hours.

Model

Lead delivery versus an owned outbound seat

VA Horizon model

VA Horizon places a dedicated cold calling VA into your wholesaling operation and manages the system around that VA. The VA's job is narrow: call, qualify, and submit qualified seller leads. VA Horizon handles list sourcing, skip tracing coordination, CRM buildout, tagging, follow-up sequences, weekly QA, call reviews, and performance management. That matters for operators who want control over call notes, recordings, lead status, and the next hire after the first caller.

Call Motivated Sellers model

Call Motivated Sellers is framed as a managed motivated-seller cold calling and lead-delivery service. That can be useful when the buyer wants the provider to handle outreach and send leads back without building a caller seat internally. The tradeoff to clarify is ownership: who controls the list criteria, how the seller is qualified, what counts as a lead, and how follow-up is handled after the first conversation.

Callers

Who makes the seller calls?

VA Horizon uses Egyptian VAs, not Filipino VAs, because the company is built around no-accent fluent English for U.S. motivated-seller conversations. Every cold calling VA must have prior real estate cold calling experience. The role is not general sales and not admin. It is distressed seller outreach, qualification, and submission into the client's CRM.

Call Motivated Sellers should be evaluated as a provider-managed calling team. The public positioning includes English and Spanish calling, which can be a real advantage in markets where Spanish seller conversations are common. The question is not whether that is useful. It is whether the investor wants bilingual lead delivery from an outside service or a dedicated caller trained inside a VA Horizon-managed operating system.

Pricing

Compare the number only after you compare what is included

VA Horizon publishes the core number: one cold calling VA is $960 per month plus $200 per month for the Readymode dialer, or $1,160 per month all-in. At three or more VAs, the VA cost drops to $800 plus the $200 dialer, or $1,000 per month per VA. HighLevel CRM buildout is included at no extra cost. The price buys staffing and the operating layer around the staffing.

For Call Motivated Sellers, verify current pricing directly with the provider. The right comparison is the full monthly scope. Ask what happens after a lead is delivered, whether call recordings are included, who pays for lists and skip tracing, how Spanish calling affects pricing, and whether follow-up is included or handed back to your team.

Onboarding

Launch speed depends on the operating model

VA Horizon is designed to put a caller live within 48 to 72 hours of engagement start. The CRM is configured before the VA starts, which means the first calls can produce usable records instead of disconnected notes. This is different from a freelance hiring path, where a wholesaler may spend two to four weeks sourcing, vetting, training, buying tools, and supervising early calls.

With Call Motivated Sellers, ask for the current onboarding sequence in writing: intake, list transfer, script approval, campaign start, lead delivery format, and first reporting date. A lead-delivery service can feel simpler up front, but launch quality depends on how clearly the seller criteria are defined before calls begin.

Guarantee

The lead definition is the contract

VA Horizon guarantees a minimum of 30 qualified leads per month per cold calling engagement. If the target is not met, VA Horizon continues dialing at no additional charge until the target is reached or places additional VAs to hit the target within the original timeframe. This guarantee works because VA Horizon controls the caller, dialer, CRM workflow, QA, and performance management.

For Call Motivated Sellers, verify current guarantee language directly. In any lead-delivery model, the key details are the seller motivation threshold, property condition notes, timeline, asking price, appointment status, duplicate handling, and refund or replacement remedy. A clear lead definition prevents a service from counting weak conversations as pipeline.

Tools

Dialer and CRM access change the day-to-day workflow

VA Horizon uses Readymode as the primary dialer. Readymode has a three to five seat minimum, which keeps many solo operators from accessing it directly. Because VA Horizon buys seats at scale, clients get Readymode access inside the cold calling package. A trained VA using this setup is expected to make 800 to 1,000 dials per 8-hour shift, with contact rates that vary by market and list quality.

VA Horizon also builds the HighLevel CRM workflow: pipeline stages, automated SMS follow-up, shared inbox, lead tagging, and task management. With Call Motivated Sellers, ask how leads enter your CRM, whether the provider supplies call recordings, and who owns follow-up after a seller raises their hand. The answer decides how much work your acquisitions team inherits.

Honest Fit

When Call Motivated Sellers is the better choice

Call Motivated Sellers is the better choice when the buyer wants motivated seller leads delivered by a managed service and does not want the operational responsibility of a dedicated caller inside their own system. It also deserves a close look when Spanish calling is a core requirement. VA Horizon's strongest offer is no-accent fluent Egyptian English-speaking VAs, Readymode access, and a managed HighLevel workflow. That is a different product than buying bilingual lead delivery.

The buyer should still verify the current details: pricing, exact lead definition, whether leads are exclusive, who supplies lists, how calls are recorded, how fast leads are delivered, and what remedy applies when lead quality misses expectations. If those terms are clear and the operator wants outsourced lead flow more than a dedicated VA system, Call Motivated Sellers can be a sensible fit.
