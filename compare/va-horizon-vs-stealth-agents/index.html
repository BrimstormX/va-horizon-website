VA Horizon vs Stealth Agents for Real Estate Wholesalers

Direct answer: For real estate wholesalers comparing VA Horizon vs Stealth Agents, the main question is specialization. Stealth Agents is best for businesses that want a general virtual assistant staffing partner across tasks such as admin support, customer service, executive assistance, marketing support, and industry-specific back-office work. VA Horizon is best for wholesalers who need a narrow outbound acquisition system built around seller conversations: dedicated Egyptian cold calling VAs, prior real estate calling experience, Readymode dialer access, HighLevel CRM buildout, QA, and a 30 qualified leads per month guarantee. Stealth Agents can make sense when your team already has scripts, managers, tools, and a documented process for the VA to follow. VA Horizon makes more sense when the missing piece is not only labor, but the managed dialer, CRM workflow, follow-up structure, and call coaching required to turn seller lists into qualified leads.

Model

General VA staffing and managed wholesaling are not the same purchase

Both options can place a person in the business, but they place responsibility in different places.

VA Horizon model

VA Horizon is built around one commercial use case: real estate wholesaling acquisition. The service includes the caller, Readymode dialer, HighLevel CRM buildout, follow-up workflow, QA, and performance management. The client does not have to become the call center manager. The VA role is intentionally narrow so the caller can spend the day on seller conversations rather than tagging records, pulling lists, writing automations, or doing admin tasks.

Stealth Agents model

Stealth Agents is a general virtual assistant staffing company. That can be useful for companies that need help across many functions, including admin, customer service, marketing support, executive assistance, or other operational tasks. A generalist staffing model gives the client flexibility, but it also means the client needs to define the role, provide tools, supervise the work, and confirm whether the assigned VA has the exact wholesaling background required.

Caller Background

Cold calling sellers requires prior real estate phone experience

A motivated seller call is not a standard receptionist call or a general sales call.

VA Horizon caller standard

VA Horizon requires prior real estate cold calling experience for cold calling placements. The callers are Egyptian, no-accent fluent in English, and trained for U.S. seller outreach. They need to ask about motivation, property condition, timeline, asking price, and next steps while keeping the call natural. That experience matters because owners often start defensive, suspicious, or impatient.

What to verify with Stealth Agents

Stealth Agents may be able to source capable VAs, but a wholesaler should verify the actual candidate's real estate cold calling history. Ask for proof of seller outreach experience, sample call recordings if available, dialer familiarity, comfort with rejection, and knowledge of wholesale lead qualification. A VA who is excellent at admin or customer service may still need substantial training before handling distressed seller conversations.

Tools

The dialer and CRM decide whether the caller can produce volume

Hiring the person without the calling system leaves too many performance variables open.

VA Horizon tool stack

VA Horizon clients receive Readymode access as part of the cold calling package. Readymode has a 3 to 5 seat minimum, which makes agency access valuable for operators who are not ready to buy multiple seats alone. VA Horizon also builds HighLevel around the calling process: pipeline stages, lead tags, SMS follow-up, shared inbox, tasks, and reporting. The caller launches into a workflow that is already designed for seller acquisition.

General staffing tool stack

With a general VA staffing service, the buyer should confirm exactly what is included. If the client must provide the dialer, phone numbers, scripts, CRM, call dispositions, lead form, and QA process, then the staffing fee is only one part of the real cost. Without a predictive dialer and clean CRM handoff, daily activity can look busy while qualified leads remain inconsistent.

Pricing

Compare all-in operating cost, not only monthly VA cost

A lower staffing price can become more expensive if the missing systems require separate vendors.

VA Horizon pricing

A single VA Horizon cold calling VA costs $1,160 per month, including $960 for the VA and $200 for the dialer. Teams with three or more cold calling VAs cost $1,000 per month per VA. HighLevel CRM buildout is included at no extra cost. Manager roles are priced separately: $1,440 per month for acquisition or disposition managers and $1,120 per month for a lead manager.

Stealth Agents pricing questions

Stealth Agents pricing and plan terms should be checked directly because VA staffing packages can change. For a fair comparison, ask whether the price includes dialer access, CRM buildout, replacement support, training, QA, reporting, and industry-specific management. If those items are not included, budget for them before comparing the final cost against a managed wholesaling service.

Management

The owner of QA owns the outcome

The biggest failure point in VA cold calling is usually management, not raw effort.

VA Horizon QA

VA Horizon reviews calls, monitors performance, and manages the VA against lead output. The caller is expected to hit high daily activity through Readymode, then submit qualified leads into HighLevel with the right notes. QA is not a one-time onboarding task. It is the weekly mechanism that catches poor openings, weak qualification, bad dispositions, and missed follow-up before the pipeline suffers.

Self-managed QA

A general staffing model can work when the client has a manager who knows what good wholesaling calls sound like. That manager must inspect call recordings, correct scripts, watch dial volume, coach objection handling, and keep CRM data clean. If nobody has time to do that, the VA may be left with vague instructions and no feedback loop.

Best Fit

Use the narrow system when the target is seller leads

The more specific the revenue target, the more valuable specialization becomes.

Choose VA Horizon when

Choose VA Horizon when the goal is seller pipeline creation for wholesaling. It is strongest for operators who want outbound calling, CRM buildout, and performance management tied together. It also fits teams planning to scale from one caller to three callers, then add an acquisition manager, disposition manager, and lead manager as qualified lead volume grows.

Choose Stealth Agents when

Choose Stealth Agents when the need is broad support and you want a VA who can follow your internal process across several task types. It may be a better fit for admin support, customer support, calendar help, research, back-office work, or non-wholesaling tasks where your internal team already has documentation and management capacity.

Honest Fit

When Stealth Agents is the better choice

Stealth Agents is the better choice when the role is not primarily real estate cold calling. If you need an executive assistant, admin coordinator, customer support rep, marketing assistant, or broad operational VA, a general staffing provider can give you a wider hiring lane than a wholesaling-specific cold calling system.

It is also a better fit when your business wants to own the process. Some operators already have a CRM, scripts, dialer, QA manager, reporting cadence, and training library. In that case, they may prefer a staffing partner that helps fill the seat while their internal manager controls the workflow.

For outbound seller acquisition, the buyer should be honest about the management burden. If you cannot review calls, manage the CRM, troubleshoot dialer problems, and coach the VA, the managed VA Horizon model is likely the cleaner fit.
