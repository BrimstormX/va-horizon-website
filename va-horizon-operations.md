# VA Horizon — Operations Notes for Blog Writing

This file documents how VA Horizon operates. Use it when writing or editing blog content, case studies, or any marketing copy to ensure accuracy and consistency.

---

## Who We Are

VA Horizon is a managed VA agency specializing in real estate wholesaling operations. We place trained virtual assistants and build out the systems (CRM, dialer, follow-up sequences) that make them productive.

---

## Our VAs

### Origin
- We source exclusively **Egyptian VAs** — not Filipino.
- Egyptian English is more neutral to U.S. ears than Filipino English, which reduces accent-related friction on cold calls and improves seller conversion rates.
- Our VAs are **no-accent fluent** — sellers in U.S. markets communicate with them without friction.

### Experience Requirement
- All cold calling VAs have **prior real estate-specific cold calling experience** — not just general sales experience.
- We do not place general VAs in cold calling roles.

---

## Cold Calling VA Role (What It Is and Isn't)

A cold calling VA placed by VA Horizon has **one job**: cold call leads, qualify sellers, and submit qualified leads.

**The VA does NOT:**
- Build or manage lists
- Skip trace leads
- Tag CRM entries
- Manage follow-up sequences
- Handle any admin work

**VA Horizon handles:**
- List sourcing and skip tracing
- CRM buildout and tagging
- Follow-up sequences (SMS/email automation)
- Weekly QA and call reviews
- Ongoing performance management

The client does not do operational admin work — that's part of what they're paying for.

---

## Dialing Numbers

- A trained VA using our dialer setup hits **800–1,000 dials per 8-hour shift**.
- Below 500 dials per day is a red flag indicating dialer or VA performance issues.
- Contact rates vary by market and list quality — typically 8–15% of dials with a predictive dialer.

---

## Dialer: Readymode

- VA Horizon uses **Readymode** as our primary dialer.
- Readymode is a predictive/power dialer purpose-built for high-volume real estate cold calling.
- **Minimum seat requirement: 3–5 seats** — individual operators hiring a single freelance VA cannot access Readymode on their own.
- Because VA Horizon buys seats at scale, our clients get access to Readymode as part of the package. This is one of the primary performance advantages of using an agency over hiring freelance.

---

## Performance Guarantee

- VA Horizon guarantees a minimum of **30 qualified leads per month** per cold calling engagement.
- If the target is not met:
  - We continue dialing at no additional charge until the target is reached, OR
  - We place additional VAs to hit the target within the original timeframe.
- This guarantee does not exist with freelance hires — there's no one accountable except the operator.

## Acquisition Manager Hiring Criteria

VA Horizon does **not** place AMs who are learning the role on client deals. Every Acquisition Manager must meet all three criteria:

1. **1+ year of cold calling experience** — they must have worked the phones themselves and understand seller psychology from the caller's seat.
2. **6+ months of vetted acquisition management experience** — specifically in real estate, managing leads from first contact through signed contract.
3. **Minimum 2 closed deals on their track record** — verified, not self-reported.

Candidates who don't meet all three criteria are not placed.

## Team Scaling Path (Recommended Sequence)

### Stage 1 — 1 Cold Caller
- Proves the model, generates initial pipeline.
- Client (the operator) handles acquisition initially.
- When lead volume outpaces acquisition capacity, move to Stage 2.

### Stage 2 — 3 Cold Callers
- First logical scale point.
- VA Horizon multi-VA discount kicks in at 3 seats.
- 3 callers at 800–1,000 dials/day generates roughly 90+ qualified leads/month.
- At this volume, one AM is needed to keep up.

### Stage 3 — Add an Acquisition Manager
- Re-qualifies leads, builds rapport, runs comps, sends offers, negotiates, and follows up until the deal is closed.
- AM works closely with cold callers — takes warm hand-offs, not cold intros.
- Market pricing: $3,500–$6,000/month (U.S.); VA Horizon pricing significantly lower.

### Stage 4 — Add a Disposition Manager
- Expands buyer's list and markets contracts.
- Works closely with the AM — dispo needs to know what buyers will pay so AM knows what to offer.
- Separating dispo from acquisition lets both roles specialize instead of each doing both at half quality.

### Stage 5 — Add a Lead Manager (Junior AM)
- Triggered when qualified lead volume exceeds what one AM can handle (typically 50+ qualified leads/month).
- Handles initial re-qualification, keeps leads warm, and hands off the highest-motivation sellers to the senior AM for closing.
- Essentially an AM in training who handles top-of-funnel acquisition conversations.

### Full Team Structure
3 cold callers → 1 Acquisition Manager → 1 Disposition Manager → 1 Lead Manager (when volume demands)

This structure can handle 4–8 deals/month without the operator being in every conversation.

## Writing Style Rules

- **No em dashes.** Do not use em dashes (—) anywhere in body copy, headings, list items, CTAs, or visible page text. This applies to all pages: blogs, case studies, landing pages, the AI automations page, and any new content going forward. Em dashes make copy feel AI-generated.
  - Replace with a period when starting a new thought.
  - Replace with a comma when continuing the same clause.
  - Replace with a colon when introducing a list, explanation, or example.
  - Replace with parentheses when adding a parenthetical aside.
  - Em dashes in meta tags, JSON-LD, and HTML title tags are acceptable since they are not visible to readers.
- **Attribution format:** Use a comma, not an em dash. Write `Ray C., Memphis, TN` not `Ray C. — Memphis, TN`.
