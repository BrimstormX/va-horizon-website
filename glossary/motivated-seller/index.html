What Is Motivated Seller?

Also known as: Distressed Seller

A motivated seller is a property owner with a pressing reason to sell quickly, often for less than full market price. Finding and qualifying motivated sellers is the core of wholesale lead generation.

Definition

A motivated seller is a property owner with a pressing reason to sell quickly, often for less than full market price. Finding and qualifying motivated sellers is the core of wholesale lead generation.

In Depth

Motivated Seller explained

Motivation is what makes a wholesale deal possible. Owners who are not motivated will hold out for full retail value, which leaves no room for a wholesale spread. Motivated sellers, by contrast, value speed, certainty, and convenience over squeezing out the last dollar.

Common drivers of motivation include foreclosure, divorce, probate or inherited property, tired landlords, costly repairs, job relocation, and tax or code issues. Wholesalers reach these sellers through cold calling, SMS, direct mail, and driving for dollars, then qualify them on a few key points: their situation, timeline, asking price, and the property's condition.

Example

An out-of-state owner inherits a house that needs $50,000 of work. They have no interest in managing a renovation or a listing, so they accept a fast cash offer below retail. That motivation is what creates the spread.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do wholesalers find motivated sellers?
Through outbound channels: cold calling and SMS to targeted lists (absentee owners, high-equity, pre-foreclosure), direct mail, and driving for dollars to spot distressed properties. High call and message volume on a good list is what surfaces motivated sellers consistently.
How do you qualify a motivated seller?
Ask about their situation and why they are selling, their timeline, their price expectation, and the property's condition. Those four answers tell you whether there is a workable deal and how to structure your offer.
